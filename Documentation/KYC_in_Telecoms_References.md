## PRIMARY LEGISLATIVE INSTRUMENTS

### United States

- **TRACED Act** — Telephone Robocall Abuse Criminal Enforcement and Deterrence Act, Pub. L. 116-105, enacted 30 December 2019. https://www.congress.gov/bill/116th-congress/senate-bill/151
- **47 CFR Part 64, Subpart HH** — Caller ID Authentication (STIR/SHAKEN). Current eCFR: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-47/chapter-I/subchapter-B/part-64/subpart-HH
- **47 CFR § 64.1200(n)(4)** — Know Your Customer obligation for voice service providers. https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-47/chapter-I/subchapter-B/part-64/section-64.1200
- **47 CFR § 64.6305** — STIR/SHAKEN implementation; Robocall Mitigation Database requirements.
- **Truth in Caller ID Act** — 47 U.S.C. § 227(e). Prohibition on spoofed caller ID with intent to defraud.
- **Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)** — 47 U.S.C. § 227. Consumer protection for automated and prerecorded calls.
- **Telemarketing Sales Rule (TSR)** — 16 CFR Part 310, especially § 310.3(b) (substantial assistance liability). https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-16/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-310
- **FTC Act § 5** — 15 U.S.C. § 45. FTC's general consumer protection authority.
- **Wire Fraud Statute** — 18 U.S.C. § 1343.
- **Conspiracy** — 18 U.S.C. § 371.
- **Computer Fraud and Abuse Act** — 18 U.S.C. § 1030.
- **Money Laundering** — 18 U.S.C. § 1956.
